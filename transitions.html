Efficient Transitions

Reduce wasted time between activities and keep students on task with smooth, predictable movements.

1. Plan the Transition

What It Is

Thinking through and clearly communicating the steps students need to take to move from one activity to the next.

What It Sounds Like

"First, put your math folder in your desk. Second, stand quietly behind your chair. Third, wait for my signal to line up."

Why It Matters

Planning the steps in advance prevents student confusion and sets clear expectations for behavior.

2. Practice, Practice, Practice

What It Is

Rehearsing the transition steps with students until they can execute them quickly and quietly.

What It Looks Like

You lead students through the transition, stopping to correct any mistakes and providing positive reinforcement.

Why It Matters

Just like with sports or music, practicing a routine builds muscle memory, making it second nature for students over time.

3. Time It

What It Is

Giving a clear time limit for the transition and sometimes using a timer to make it a game.

What It Looks Like

You might say, "Let's see if we can all get to the carpet in under 30 seconds!" and start a timer.

Why It Matters

Timing adds an element of fun and challenge, encouraging students to stay focused and work together to meet the goal.
