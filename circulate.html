The Circulate Technique

Beyond the door, your movement through the classroom can be a powerful tool. Learn how to actively "Circulate" to support student learning and classroom management.

Position for Power

What It Is

Deliberately moving to different parts of the room to manage behavior and keep students engaged.

What It Looks Like

You walk to the back of the room, stand near a talkative group, or move between desks as students work.

What It Sounds Like

"I see a group in the back working hard on their project." (said as you move toward them)

Why It Matters

Your presence acts as a non-verbal cue that you are aware of what is happening everywhere in the classroom, reducing off-task behavior.

Scan and React

What It Is

Continuously scanning the room to monitor student understanding and quickly address issues.

What It Looks Like

Your head is up, eyes moving across the room, and you offer a thumbs-up or gentle tap on the shoulder to redirect.

What It Sounds Like

"Nice work, Sarah! You got it." (said in a low voice to a student who's struggling but then figures it out)

Why It Matters

This allows you to provide real-time feedback and support, preventing small problems from escalating.

Engage with Students

What It Is

Interacting directly with individual students or small groups during independent work.

What It Looks Like

You squat down next to a student's desk, look at their work, and have a quiet conversation about their progress.

What It Sounds Like

"Tell me what you think the next step should be." or "I love the way you used a complete sentence there!"

Why It Matters

This builds stronger relationships and provides personalized academic support, making students feel more connected and motivated.
