Build a strong, respectful classroom community by working with students to establish shared expectations.

1. Explain the "Why"

What It Is

Sharing the importance of a respectful and productive learning environment with students.

What It Sounds Like

"We're a team in this classroom. When we all agree on how to treat each other, everyone feels safe and can do their best work."

Why It Matters

This sets a clear purpose and helps students feel more invested in the process from the beginning.

2. Brainstorm Together

What It Is

Inviting students to share their ideas for classroom rules or agreements.

What It Looks Like

You write student suggestions on chart paper or a whiteboard, creating a list of ideas for how to behave in the classroom.

What It Sounds Like

"What do we need to do to make sure everyone feels heard and respected?"

Why It Matters

This empowers students by giving them a voice and ensures the agreements are relevant to their needs.

3. Group & Refine

What It Is

Working with students to combine similar ideas and simplify them into clear, positive statements.

What It Looks Like

You guide students to turn "No running" and "No hitting" into a single, positive agreement like "We walk with our bodies calm and kind."

Why It Matters

Consolidating the ideas creates a concise, easy-to-remember list of expectations that focuses on what to do, not what to avoid.

4. Commit & Display

What It Is

Formally agreeing to the final list and displaying it in the classroom.

What It Looks Like

Students sign the final poster, which is then placed in a prominent, visible location.

Why It Matters

This act solidifies student ownership and provides a constant visual reminder of their commitments to each other.
