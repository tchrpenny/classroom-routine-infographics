1. Be Prepared

What It Is

Having your classroom and materials ready *before* students arrive.

2. Stand at the Door

What It Is

Physically positioning yourself at the classroom entrance.

What It Looks Like

You are standing in the doorway with a welcoming stance as students approach.

Why It Matters

This signals a welcoming presence and helps you monitor the tone of the classroom entry.

3. Greet Each Student

What It Is

Offering a positive, personal welcome to every student as they enter the room.

What It Looks Like

You make eye contact, smile, and maybe offer a high-five or a handshake.

What It Sounds Like

"Good morning, Maya! I'm so glad you're here."

Why It Matters

It makes each student feel seen, valued, and ready to learn.

4. Give a Direction

What It Is

Providing a clear and simple instruction for what students should do after they enter.

It prevents wandering and sets a clear routine, helping students transition smoothly into the day.
