1. The Signal

What It Is

A consistent, pre-taught cue to get students' attention.

What It Looks Like

You raise your hand, clap a rhythmic pattern, or ring a small bell.

What It Sounds Like

"Class, class?" with the students responding "Yes, yes!"

Why It Matters

A clear signal reduces the need to raise your voice and helps students quickly recognize when it's time to listen.

2. The Pause

What It Is

A moment of silence after the signal is given, waiting for 100% of students to be quiet and still.

What It Looks Like

You hold your hand in the air and stand still, making eye contact with students as they quiet down.

Why It Matters

This teaches students that you expect full attention and gives them a moment to self-correct before you begin speaking.

3. The Follow-Through

What It Is

Beginning your instruction or message only after you have achieved complete stillness and quiet.

What It Looks Like

Once the room is silent, you make a clear statement like, "Thank you for getting ready. Please put your pencils down and track the speaker."

Why It Matters

Consistency reinforces the importance of the signal, ensuring students know that when you give the cue, it's time to listen.
