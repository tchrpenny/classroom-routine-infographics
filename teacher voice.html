Strategies for an Engaging Teacher Voice

Your voice is one of your most powerful teaching tools. Learn how to use it to keep students focused and engaged.

1. Use Strategic Pauses

What It Is

Using brief moments of silence to emphasize a point or get students to listen closely.

What It Sounds Like

"The most important rule in our classroom is... [pause]... to always be kind."

Why It Matters

A well-timed pause creates anticipation and signals that what you are about to say is very important.

2. Vary Your Intonation

What It Is

Changing the pitch of your voice to add emotion, highlight key words, and prevent a monotone delivery.

What It Sounds Like

You might raise your voice on the word "excited" to match the emotion: "I'm so EXCITED for this new book!"

Why It Matters

A dynamic voice keeps students engaged and makes it easier for them to remember key information.

3. Be a Word Economist

What It Is

Using the fewest words possible to give instructions and explanations.

What It Sounds Like

Instead of, "Okay, everyone, please try to get your pencils out and open your notebooks," you say, "Pencils out. Notebooks open."

Why It Matters

Clear, concise instructions reduce student confusion and help them follow directions more easily.
