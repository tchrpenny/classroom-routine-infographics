CHAMPS Strategy

This framework helps you define clear expectations for every classroom activity, ensuring students know what success looks like.

C: Conversation

What It Is

Defining when and how students can talk during an activity.

What It Looks Like

You display a sign that says "C: Level 0" for silent work or "C: Level 2" for quiet partner talk.

Why It Matters

This helps students understand when it is appropriate to speak and prevents disruptive chatter.

H: Help

What It Is

Clarifying how students can ask for assistance when they need it.

What It Looks Like

Students raise a hand quietly or use a "Help" cup on their desk to signal they need you.

Why It Matters

This establishes a clear, consistent routine so students aren't calling out for help, which can interrupt others.

A: Activity

What It Is

Defining the task students are currently working on.

What It Looks Like

You state, "Your activity is to complete problems 1-5 on the worksheet."

Why It Matters

This ensures students are clear about the learning goal and what they should be focused on.

M: Movement

What It Is

Setting clear expectations for when and where students can move during an activity.

What It Looks Like

You state, "M: Stay at your seat" or "M: You may walk to the pencil sharpener and back."

Why It Matters

This prevents students from wandering and helps maintain an orderly classroom environment.

P: Participation

What It Is

Explaining how students will show they are engaged and on task.

What It Looks Like

You state, "P: Everyone will write their answer down on their whiteboard" or "P: Each team member will contribute one idea."

Why It Matters

This holds students accountable for their learning and provides a clear metric for on-task behavior.

S: Success

What It Is

A statement that describes what a successful outcome looks like.

What It Looks Like

You say, "When you are done, you will have completed a paragraph with at least three complete sentences that describe your favorite animal."

Why It Matters

This provides a clear goal for students to work toward, helping them feel successful when they meet the expectation.
